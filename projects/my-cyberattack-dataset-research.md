# My Cyberattack Dataset Research

## What This Project Is About

This project is about researching and organizing large cybersecurity incident datasets, including ransomware attacks, public-sector attacks, and other cyber incidents affecting organizations, governments, schools, and communities.

The goal was to help create a more reliable ground truth dataset that researchers can use to understand where cyberattacks happened, what type of attack occurred, and who was affected.

## Why I Worked On It

Cyber incident data is often messy. The same attack may appear in different sources with different names, dates, locations, or descriptions.

I worked on this project to help organize cyberattack information into a cleaner and more useful structure for future analysis.

## What I Worked On

- Large-scale cyber incident research
- Ransomware attack records

- Data cleaning and structuring
- Matching attacks to organizations and communities
- Identifying attack types
- Organizing incident timelines
- Supporting a ground truth dataset for cybersecurity research

## Research Questions

- What organization or community was affected?
- What type of cyberattack happened?
- When did the attack happen?
- What sector was targeted?
- Was the attack ransomware, phishing, DDoS, data breach, or another type?
- What public evidence supports the record?
- How can this record be matched to other data sources?

## Example Dataset Fields

| Field | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Organization | Name of the affected organization |
| Community | City, county, school district, or local area |
| State or Country | Geographic location |
| Sector | Education, government, healthcare, transit, law enforcement, or other |
| Attack Type | Ransomware, breach, DDoS, phishing, malware, or other |
| Date | Known or reported incident date |
| Source | Public source used for verification |
| Confidence | How reliable the match appears |
