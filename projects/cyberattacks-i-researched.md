# Cyberattacks I Researched

## What This Project Is About

This project was about researching cyberattacks across public institutions in the United States, including cities, schools, police departments, transit systems, and other public organizations.

I worked with data from many U.S. states and looked for evidence of where cyberattacks happened, what organizations were affected, and whether nearby communities or related institutions had similar incidents.

## Why I Made It

Cyberattacks do not only affect computers. They affect real communities, schools, public services, transportation, and local governments.

I wanted to understand how cyber incidents appear across different places and how researchers can connect public information into a clearer picture.

## What I Worked On

- Cyberattack research across many U.S. states
- Public-sector incident analysis
- Matching affected cities, schools, and nearby communities
- Data cleaning and organization
- Research validation
- Looking for patterns in cyber incidents

## Skills I Practiced

- Cyber incident research
- Threat analysis
- Data matching
- Public-source research
- Security analysis

## Important Privacy Note

This project should only include public information, redacted examples, or safe sample data. I do not publish private research data, confidential school data, employer information, or personal information.

## What I Learned

I learned that cyberattack research requires patience, verification, and careful documentation. The same incident can appear in many different ways, so it is important to check sources and organize information clearly.

This project helped me understand how cyber incidents connect to real communities and public services.
